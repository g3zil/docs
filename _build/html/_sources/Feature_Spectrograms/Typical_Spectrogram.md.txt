# Typical Spectrogram

**Title:** Typical Spectrogram

**Contributed by:** Bob Mattaliano N6RFM

**Reviewed by:**

**Receiving Station Location:** EM12jw, near Fort Worth, TX 16January2026

**Receiving Station Details:** Grape-1 DRF

**Feature Description**

![WWV 10MHz 16January2026](/_static/images/image001.jpg)

Typical GRAPE spectrogram measuring propagation on 10 MHz from WWV to EM12jw over a 24-hour period. The most prominent features (darker red lines) are the reception path closing (A) at ~0045Z (~one hour after local sunset) and opening ~1345Z (~15 minutes after local sunrise). Details regarding other spectrogram features will be described in the other examples.
